Espresso

Espresso is the extraction of coffee through heat and pressure. The end result is a uniquely smooth brew.

The perfection of espresso coffee has deep roots and precise scientific and technological reasons. From growing the plant and treating the beans, to preservation, production and consumption, each step of coffeeâ€™s lifecycle is important to pulling the perfect shot.

Drip

Drip coffee can be achieved through a number of methods. The simple cone filter with hot water poured over the grounds is a classic and effective brewing method. There is still a bit of technique involved with creating an amazing pour over coffee.

When the correct temperature and brew time are achieved, the results are fabulous. Pour over coffee can be an incredibly delicious drink.

Roast Profiles and Brewing

Espresso

For pulling espresso shots, we typically follow the Italian roast profile for the beans we use. This means that the roast is typically darker. This helps take away the sour flavor of light roasts. Darker roasts typically pull a rich and smooth espresso shot.

Drip

We use the lighter roasted beans for our pour over brews. Pour over matches well with the light flavor profile of our lighter roasted coffee beans. We can always subsititute darker beans for your pour over brew.
